What a metaphor is

A catalyst that selects a coordination the body already affords.

Metaphor describes one thing in the terms of another. Lakoff and Johnson (1980) map one whole domain onto another, $A \to B$. The reading here restricts the source, sending a discrete subset $A'$ of its features onto the full range of a target $B'$, since a coordination draws on only the few features that constrain it. Those features shape the coordination that follows.

$$A' \to B', \qquad A' \subsetneq A\ \text{(discrete)}, \quad B' \subseteq B$$

Sitting and enacted metaphor

SITTING

run in the mind

$f:A'\to B'$, inferential and analogical, as in “I feel like a million bucks”

ENACTED

acted out in the body

$\mathrm{image}:X\mapsto X_{\text{image}}\subseteq X$, restricting the space of coordinations

Sitting metaphors reason from source to target, tracing the mapping $f:A'\to B'$ in the mind. An enacted metaphor comes into being as the body performs it, since acting the image out brings the coordination into play. Raising a banana to her ear, a child enacts $\mathrm{Aff}(\text{banana})\cap\mathrm{Act}(\text{phone})$, the intersection of the two, the banana keeping its own affordances throughout, so that “one sees in the banana the affordance of a particular sort of action” (Gallagher and Lindgren 2015, 397).

Metaphor as catalyst

A catalyst lowers the barrier to a reaction and emerges from it unchanged. The teaching image works the same way, restricting the space of possible coordinations to a subset the body already affords, $X\mapsto X_{\text{image}}\subseteq X$, so that one settled coordination becomes the cheapest to reach. Each domain keeps its own affordances, the learner realizing the selected relation in its own joints.

The metaphor term $\beta\,L_{\mathrm{metaphor}}$ adds signed couplings over the joints of the selected subset $A'$, collapsing the movement onto the manifold $X_{\text{image}}$ those couplings allow. Fixing a phase relation between limbs and leaving the joint values free, the couplings let two differently built bodies settle into one coordination, each in its own coordinates, since the coordination is the relation the couplings fix, which each body meets with its own joints (Haken, Kelso and Bunz 1985).

The formalism

A body is a set of coupled joints. All the coordination lives in one matrix.

A pose is a vector of joint angles $x \in \mathbb{R}^{n}$. The body’s coordination is an energy, and comfortable postures are more likely:

$$\begin{gathered} E(x)=\tfrac{1}{2}\,x^{\top}J x \qquad p(x)\propto e^{-E/T} \\[2pt] \Rightarrow\quad x\sim\mathcal{N}(0,\Sigma),\qquad \Sigma=T\,J^{-1} \end{gathered}$$
In plain terms: lower-effort postures are more likely, so movements scatter in a bell-shaped cloud whose shape is set entirely by one table of numbers, J (the inverse of the covariance).

Anatomy and metaphor in one matrix

J splits three ways, and all three are signed graph Laplacians, so one construction carries the anatomy and the metaphor together.

$$\begin{gathered} J=\gamma I+L_{\mathrm{anatomy}}+\beta\,L_{\mathrm{metaphor}} \\[2pt] =\ \gamma I+L\!\left(\mathrm{anatomy}^{+}\cup\ \beta\,\mathrm{metaphor}^{\pm}\right) \end{gathered}$$

For a signed, weighted edge set $W$, the Laplacian is $L=\bar{D}-W$ with the absolute-degree diagonal $\bar{D}_{ii}=\sum_{j}\lvert W_{ij}\rvert$. That absolute value is what keeps J positive-definite even when an edge is negative.

  • $\gamma I$ — a baseline stiffness; each joint resists moving on its own.
  • $L_{\mathrm{anatomy}}$ — the kinematic tree: the bones, as positive edges.
  • $\beta\,L_{\mathrm{metaphor}}$ — the teaching image: signed edges linking limbs, $\beta$ the dial.

How compressed the motion is

The covariance $\Sigma$ carries one variance $\lambda_{i}$ per independent direction of movement. The participation ratio turns that spectrum into a count of the directions actually in play:

$$\operatorname{PR}(\Sigma)=\frac{\left(\sum_{i}\lambda_{i}\right)^{2}}{\sum_{i}\lambda_{i}^{2}}\ \in\ [1,\,n]$$
In plain terms: $\mathrm{PR}=n$ means every joint does its own thing; $\mathrm{PR}\to 1$ means the whole body rides a single synergy. And since $\Sigma=T\,J^{-1}$ scales every $\lambda_{i}$ by the same $T$, temperature cannot change PR — dimensionality lives in J, variability lives in $T$. A metaphor compresses the motion; practice only tightens its spread.

What a coupling’s sign fixes

A single teaching image is one coupling, $E=\tfrac{1}{2}\,w\,(a\,x_{i}-b\,x_{j})^{2}$, and its perfect-square expansion is the Laplacian read with a sign: the $w a^{2},\ w b^{2}$ diagonal is $\bar{D}$ and the $-w a b$ off-diagonal is $-W$. Summing the squares over anatomy + metaphor rebuilds the one signed Laplacian — signs are not a second mechanism bolted on.

POSITIVE EDGE

in-phase

ordinary block $\bar{D}-W$ · joints move together (0°)

NEGATIVE EDGE

anti-phase

signless block $\bar{D}+\lvert W\rvert$ · joints mirror (180°)

Off-diagonal $J < 0$ is in-phase; $J > 0$ is anti-phase. Fixed ratio $(1,r)$ is the same perfect square at unequal coefficients. One builder — no spin model imported.

Recovering the couplings from motion how it’s coded

Given real motion-capture, the model runs backward — it reads the couplings out of the movement, then splits them by graph support.

  1. Recover. Take the empirical covariance $\hat{\Sigma}$ of the joint angles across frames and invert it, $\hat{J}=\hat{\Sigma}^{-1}$. The couplings come from the data, nothing assumed.
  2. Decompose. On each bone edge, the anatomical weight is the recovered partial coupling, $w_{\mathrm{anat}}=-\hat{J}_{ij}$; build $L_{\mathrm{anat}}$ from those, then $M=\hat{J}-L_{\mathrm{anat}}$. $M$ vanishes on the tree and carries the metaphor off-tree.
  3. Read. The sign of each off-tree entry is the relation: $M_{ij} < 0$ in-phase, $M_{ij} > 0$ anti-phase — limb to limb.
Verified: on synthetic data a planted metaphor comes back exactly ($\hat{J}$ recovered to $10^{-9}$), the tree-edge residual is 0, and $M$’s sign flips with the task.

The model

What the analogy actually does — and what it does not.

What one constraint does

With one constraint, an analogy sets which coordination the body takes — not how compressed the motion is, but which pattern it falls into.

NOT

how compressed the motion is

dimensionality — it stays fixed

BUT

which coordination it takes

the pattern — it flips with the task

Two differently built bodies fall into the same coordination, each reaching it through its own joints. That settled pattern is the order parameter (Haken, Kelso & Bunz, 1985).

The teaching image is a signed edge

Frustration

Give the body two images that fight — one “together,” one “mirror” — at strengths $W(1-\lambda)$ and $W\lambda$ on the same pair. The shared off-diagonal is their signed sum:

$$J_{ij}=-W(1-\lambda)+W\lambda=-W(1-2\lambda)$$

At $\lambda=\tfrac12$ the two demands are equal, the coupling passes through zero, and coordination freezes rather than averaging into a compromise. So the order and compatibility of teaching images should matter for learning.

Key terms

In plain language.

Degrees of freedom
the independent ways the body can move.
Synergy
many joints yoked to move as one.
Covariance
how much two joints move together.
Precision matrix (J)
the inverse of covariance; which joints are directly coupled.
Graph Laplacian
turns a network of couplings into an energy that scores how much connected joints disagree.
Signed edge
a coupling that is “+” (move together) or “−” (mirror).
In-phase / anti-phase
moving together (0°) versus opposing (180°).
Order parameter
one number for the whole coordination pattern.
Participation ratio (PR)
the effective number of independent directions the motion uses, (Σλ)²/Σλ².
Partial correlation
two joints’ coupling with all others held fixed.
Relative phase / phase-locking
where two limbs are in their cycle; how steadily they hold it.
Frustration
two conflicting images cancel, freezing the motion.
AUC
a 0.5–1 score: 0.5 = chance, 1 = perfect recovery.
Positive-definite
a stability condition: the energy has one clear bottom.
