Methodology, the coordination model

The modelling strand of the project, traced from inception to intended outcome. What the model is, where it comes from, why it takes the form it does, whether its premises survive contact with real movement, and the experiment it opens.

1 · inception2 · what it claims3 · the formalism4 · fit to real bodies5 · limits6 · intended outcome

1. Inception, and why this model

The project, shared across five collaborators, asks how tacit, embodied skill passes from teacher to learner when the knowledge resists telling, the problem Polanyi poses once it enters movement (Polanyi, 1966; Miton & DeDeo, 2022). A metaphor transmits it. When a teacher offers an image such as pearls on a string or cloud hands, it projects a relational structure onto the learner’s body, a projection that runs the operation analogy theory calls structure-mapping (Gentner, 1983) in a register where the mover feels the aligned structure and never stops to judge it. Its corpus side, the topic map and the bridge dossier, already establishes that the seam runs through the literature. Neither can exhibit the mechanism in a body, or say what it means concretely for a metaphor to constrain movement.

We commit to metaphor and keep analogy for the theory beneath it. A tai-chi teacher utters a metaphor, most often an A is B image, seldom a four-term proportion, so the data warrant the word metaphor. Structure-mapping stays on as the account of a metaphor’s relational content, worked in the register the embodied-metaphor tradition treats as sensorimotor where analogy theory leaves it amodal (Gentner, 1988; Abrahamson, 2020; Kimmel et al., 2024). The noun is metaphor, the operation structure-mapping.

Our modelling strand began as a first attempt at that mechanism, a rough model that writes a metaphor as a set of couplings on the body’s degrees of freedom. It draws on three traditions. Gaussian graphical models and the Ising line from statistical physics contribute the couplings, their signs, and the frustration between conflicting constraints; Bernstein’s degrees-of-freedom problem from motor control frames the joint excess a skill has to tame; Gentner’s structure-mapping fixes the relational content that must survive the mapping. As a first pass the model stayed partial. Whether its approach was worth keeping or belonged in the bin remained open.

Introducing it here tests the model against real data. We state its formalism cleanly enough to judge, fit it to real motion-capture, and mark exactly where it still fails. The first attempt comes out wrong in execution and right in its bones. Its three premises, that movement runs low-dimensional, that coordination tracks anatomy, and that relational content lives in the sign of coupling, each survive contact with real movement (§4). A second version earns its keep on that showing, which the limits (§5) and intended outcome (§6) go on to specify.

One decision from the first attempt earns keeping intact, the one the rest of the page turns on. Two knobs that do not interfere. Coupling structure sets how many dimensions of movement survive; temperature sets only how far they spread. Holding the two apart keeps separate two claims the literature routinely runs together, that a metaphor constrains coordination and that practice reduces variability.

2. What the model claims, before the algebra

A body has many joints, hence many degrees of freedom (DOF). The model casts a body as a probability distribution over joint configurations, controlled by the two knobs named above.

  • Coupling structure (the matrix J) controls the effective dimensionality of movement, how many independent directions the body actually explores. A metaphor adds couplings, binding otherwise-independent joints into a synergy and collapsing the dimension.
  • Temperature (the scalar T) controls variability only, the overall spread of movement, with the shape of coordination left untouched.

Because the two knobs stay mathematically orthogonal, the model keeps two easily-confused claims apart. Constraint on coordination lives in J; reduction of variability lives in T. The formalism in Section 3 renders that separation exact and adds signs so a coupling encodes relational content beyond mere binding. Section 4 then asks whether real bodies behave the way the model needs.

3. The formalism

State and energy

Write x ∈ ℝn for the vector of joint-angle deviations from a fixed neutral pose, one scalar per DOF. A symmetric positive-definite precision matrix J fixes a configuration’s cost as a quadratic energy.

E(x) = ½ x⊤J x

Distribution

At temperature T, a Boltzmann distribution over that energy yields a zero-mean Gaussian whose covariance inverts the precision and scales with T.

p(x) ∝ exp(−E(x)/T)  ⇒  x ~ 𝒩(0, Σ),  Σ = T J−1

The whole content of the movement lives in the covariance Σ, in how joints co-vary, since the mean pose stays fixed at neutral. This placement sets the model’s first substantive commitment. Joint-angle targets pick out a point, where a metaphor picks out a shape of covariation and suits the task a bare list of angles cannot.

Coupling structure from a graph Laplacian

A baseline stiffness plus a weighted graph Laplacian of the coupling graph forms the precision matrix. For an edge set with weights, the Laplacian L = D − W subtracts adjacency from degree; adding γI grants every DOF a finite variance on its own and keeps J positive-definite.

J = γI + Lanatomy + β Lmetaphor  L PSD, γ > 0 ⇒ J ≻ 0

Here Lanatomy stands for the body’s own linkage graph (a weak, fixed scaffold), β for the strength of the imposed metaphor, and Lmetaphor for a clique over the joints the metaphor binds. Metaphor edges drive the bound joints to co-vary and collapse movement onto a lower-dimensional manifold, a synergy.

Effective dimensionality, and why temperature cannot touch it

Effective DOF is read off the covariance spectrum {λi} by the participation ratio.

PR(Σ) = (∑i λi)2∑i λi2  ∈  [1, n]

PR = n when variance spreads equally across every direction (no coordination); PR → 1 when one collective coordinate dominates (a single synergy). Because Σ = T J−1 multiplies every eigenvalue by the same T, the ratio stays unchanged, so raising the temperature inflates the movement without reshaping it. The model earns its purpose in that exact separation, dimensionality a property of J alone, variability a property of T alone.

Signs: from binding to relation

A plain Laplacian can only bind joints to move together (positive correlation). For a metaphor to specify the relation joints keep, beyond merely that they coordinate, each coupling term takes the form of a penalty on a signed linear combination.

Eterm = ½ w (a xi − b xj)2

Its contribution to the precision matrix reads ΔJii = wa2, ΔJjj = wb2, ΔJij = −wab. The sign of the product ab sets the relation. Because every term forms a perfect square, each stays positive-semidefinite, so J remains positive-definite however many enter.

This construction yields the model’s second substantive commitment. The participation ratio runs sign-blind, since an anti-phase synergy collapses exactly as many dimensions as an in-phase one. Their difference lies in the pattern of the surviving collective mode, the correlation-sign structure. PR then tracks a metaphor’s magnitude of constraint (how much collapse, answering Bernstein). Signs fix its relational content (which coordination, where the analogy’s structure lives). Structure-mapping runs here in a motor register, where attributes (specific angles) wash out under sampling and only relations (the couplings) survive.

Frustration: conflicting metaphors freeze the body

Two metaphors that demand incompatible relations on the same joints resist joint satisfaction. Load an in-phase coupling at weight W(1−λ) and an anti-phase coupling at weight Wλ on the same pair. The off-diagonal then takes the signed sum. Each diagonal gains from both.

Jij = −W(1−λ) + Wλ = −W(1−2λ)  → 0 at λ = ½, while Jii, Jjj each gain W

At λ = ½ the coupling passes through zero while both joints stiffen, so the collective coordinates c± = (xL ± xR)/√2 both go stiff, leaving no cheap coordinated motion. Conflicting instructions never average. They cancel, so coordination collapses through zero. A Gaussian energy turns the cancellation into the loss of a soft mode, not the rugged degeneracy of a true spin glass, yet the movement consequence holds firm. Incompatible teaching images degrade coordination. No blended compromise appears.

4. Does a real body behave this way? A proof of concept

The formalism assumes three premises about real movement. Movement runs genuinely low-dimensional; its conditional-dependence structure follows anatomy; the sign of coordination changes across different movements at similar dimensionality. A fit of the Gaussian graphical model to CMU Graphics Lab motion-capture data tests each one, taking per-frame joint Euler angles with global position and orientation removed, centring and pooling trials within a task, and estimating covariance and precision across frames. Three tasks, one subject each, two trials apiece (walk, run, jump), 50 rotational DOF after dropping near-constant joints.

(A) Movement is low-dimensional

We report two participation ratios because they answer different questions. A few large-amplitude joints dominate the raw-covariance PR, which overstates the collapse; the standardized (correlation) PR measures more honestly how many coordinated directions stay active. Even standardized, roughly five dimensions out of fifty account for the movement, in line with the synergy literature, where a few principal components absorb most of the postural variance (Santello et al., 1998; Turvey, 2007).

(C) Same body, different task, the sign flips

Here the decisive result appears. Across tasks, the correlation of the matched flexion axis of paired limbs reorganizes while the effective dimensionality barely moves. Walking and running couple the limbs anti-phase (they alternate); jumping couples them in-phase (they extend together). Same collapse, opposite relational content, the sign-blind-PR claim in real human movement.

The model earns its premises. It does not yet earn the claim that a metaphor transmits a skill, since a task only proxies for an instruction, with a single body cast as teacher and learner at once. Section 6 aims exactly there.

5. Honest limits

  • Task proxies for instruction. Walk, run, and jump differ in coordination, though no metaphor enters. The distinctive claim, that a verbal image reshapes J, needs a study that varies the instruction while holding the task fixed.
  • Sign conventions stay axis-relative. Only the relative sign structure admits interpretation (contralateral opposite to ipsilateral, walk opposite to jump), never the absolute sign of any single entry.
  • Representation mismatch. The model uses one scalar angle per joint, where motion capture records three-DOF joint rotations in a true articulated chain. A fit requires either projecting each joint onto its principal rotation axis or lifting the model to three-DOF joints, a real methods decision, no mere detail.
  • Temperature models expertise only thinly. T scales all directions equally, where expert variance reduction runs anisotropic (the uncontrolled-manifold finding; Scholz & Schöner, 1999). Anisotropic reduction reshapes J, so “the learner consolidated the metaphor” and “the learner became expert” may coincide as one event in this model, a feature to claim deliberately or a conflation to resolve.
  • Small samples. The run condition offers few frames, so the fit works as a proof of concept, short of an estimate with error bars.

6. Intended outcome, from working model to fundable experiment

Everything to this point establishes a coherent mechanism, consistent with real movement. A metaphor still has to prove that it transmits a skill from one body to another, the project’s actual thesis. Proving it belongs in a lab. The steps split cleanly. Computational ones cost nothing but time and run now. One of them turns the model into an experiment worth funding.

  1. Two-body transmission, computational, run it now. Assign a teacher and a learner different bodies (different baseline J, different segment lengths). A joint-angle instruction stays body-specific and dies at the mismatch; a relational coupling (the sign-and-ratio pattern) stays body-invariant and should survive it. If the metaphor reconstructs the teacher’s synergy where a DOF list fails, the model stops illustrating that metaphor could transmit tacit skill and starts arguing that it does. The step needs no new data. It anchors the pitch below.
  2. A metaphor-instruction experiment. ◆ DISI PITCH Here we ask DISI for a real experiment. Record full-body kinematics under varied metaphorical instruction, with expert↔novice or teacher↔learner pairs, in a metaphor-native pedagogy, tai chi first, then dance, physical therapy, instrumental posture. The model arrives pre-registered. It predicts the direction of the PR collapse, the correlation-sign pattern, and the cross-body transfer, and it hands the analysis pipeline over in advance. One funded run would test that specification, nothing open-ended. Three things put it within funding reach now, the preliminary results already in hand (this page, plus step 1); the effect already on record (analogy drives implicit motor learning, Liao & Masters, 2001; instruction and attention reshape motor performance, Wulf & Lewthwaite, 2016); and the data collection already feasible, since Tits et al. (2018) captured expert Taijiquan motion at exactly this grain. Relevance to the funder writes itself for DISI, embodied skill as a non-symbolic intelligence that moves between agents by metaphor, without touching the project’s own framing.
  3. Frustration as curriculum. Test the prediction that two conflicting teaching images degrade coordination and never blend into compromise. The prediction targets the ordering and compatibility of instruction, squarely a transmission-of-learning result, and a natural second study once the first wins funding.
  4. Scale the premises up. Refit the dimensionality and anatomy results on a large corpus, AMASS or the KIT Whole-Body Human Motion Database, using distinct activities as a first, weak proxy for distinct relational content. A tai-chi–specific set already waits in the reading list (Tits et al., 2018, expert Taijiquan mocap), so feasibility poses no obstacle.
  5. Bridge the representation. Commit to either the scalar-axis projection or the three-DOF lift, so the model and the data speak the same geometry and the fit earns its rigour beyond illustration.

The model runs a Gaussian graphical model over body degrees of freedom, with a graph-Laplacian precision matrix, an in-phase / anti-phase / fixed-ratio signed-coupling extension inherited from the Ising/spin-glass line, and a temperature that scales variability without reshaping coordination. Its target quantity comes from Bernstein’s degrees-of-freedom problem, its commitment to relational content from Gentner’s structure-mapping. A proof-of-concept run fits Gaussian graphical models to CMU Graphics Lab Motion Capture (subjects walking, running, jumping), with participation ratio for effective dimensionality, partial correlations for the conditional-dependence graph, and matched-axis correlations for the sign of limb coordination. Code and data fetch live in the repository under model/, reproducible. The theoretical frame, analogy as structure-mapping meeting embodied skill, appears in the bridge dossier, and the source-gathering method behind the corpus appears in What is DATM.
